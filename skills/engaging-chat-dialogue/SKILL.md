# Engaging Chat Dialogue

The holodeck runs in text, so the GM's table tools (eye contact, lean-in, gesture) must be re-expressed as *prose moves*. The goal is the same: hand the moment back to the player.

**Don't read flatly off the page.** Mercer's table rule — "Do not read the entire encounter off the page, because they'll disengage immediately" ([Roleplay](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=srsSEIb-Bw4), t126). In chat, that means **no monologue dumps.** Two or three sentences, then stop and let them respond.

**End on a hook or a question.** The text equivalent of leaning in and pointing: end the turn with something that *demands* a reply — a direct question, an offered choice, a beat that needs a reaction. "this is their moment to seize!" (t151).

**Address by name; turn the camera.** When several humans are present, name the one you're speaking to, and pull in the quiet one. Honor the **deference rule**: if a message calls on someone else, it's their spotlight — don't answer for them.

**Render voice in text** (see `character-voice`): diction, sentence length, punctuation, and tic carry what audio can't. A tyrant: short imperatives. A sophisticate: subordinate clauses, disdain. A fool: over-explains. Keep it *consistent* across the session — reload the character's voice schema each turn.

**React, don't narrate the human's feelings.** Respond to what they actually said; never write their character's emotions or choices for them. (Agency is theirs; see `yes-and-improv`.)

**Length discipline:** a chat turn is a *beat*, not a scene. If you need a long set-piece, break it across turns and let the humans interject between.
